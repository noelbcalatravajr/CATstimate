ℹ How to use — Linear cuts ▼

What it does

  1. Enter your stock bar length — the full length of each bar, rod, or pipe you are buying.
  2. Set a lead trim if you always make a fresh cut at the start of each bar (e.g. 5–10mm).
  3. Set the kerf — the width of material lost per saw cut (typically 2–4mm).
  4. Add each cut piece — description, length, and quantity.
  5. Click Calculate. The optimiser fits pieces onto the fewest bars, longest first.
  6. Toggle Collate identical bars to group matching cut patterns.
iWaste % turning amber means over 20% waste — try adjusting your stock length.

ℹ How to use — Baluster spacing ▼

What it does

  1. Set your stock bar length, lead trim, and kerf.
  2. Choose whether your gap inputs are clear (face to face) or centre to centre.
  3. Add one section per continuous balustrade run (e.g. Stair 1, Landing, Stair 2).
  4. Enter the run length, baluster width, recommended gap, max gap, and cut length.
  5. The calculator finds the number of balusters keeping the gap as close to recommended as possible without exceeding the max.
  6. All sections combine into one cut optimiser across your stock bars.
iAS 1657 and NCC Section B specify a maximum clear gap of 125mm for residential balustrades. Confirm with your certifier.

ℹ How to use — Sheet / panel cuts ▼

What it does

  1. Enter your sheet size (width × height) and kerf.
  2. Add each panel — description, width, height, and quantity.
  3. Set rotation per panel: Free ↻ allows 90° rotation to improve fit. Locked 🔒 keeps orientation fixed — use for timber grain, brushed metal, or patterned surfaces.
  4. Click Calculate. Panels are nested using guillotine bin packing.
!Always lock rotation for materials with a visible grain, texture direction, or repeating pattern.
